# Exercise 7: Dynamics with Friction

## Problem

* A 5 kg block sits on top of a 10 kg block
* The 5 kg block is tied to a wall (so it does not move)
* A force of 45 N pulls the 10 kg block horizontally
* Coefficient of kinetic friction: μ = 0.2

Find:
→ Acceleration of the 10 kg block

---

# Step 1: Understand the Situation

* The **10 kg block moves**
* The **5 kg block stays fixed**
* Therefore, friction acts between:

  1. The two blocks
  2. The 10 kg block and the ground

---

# Step 2: Formula for Friction

F_friction = μN

Where:

* μ = coefficient of friction
* N = normal force

---

# Step 3: Friction Between the Two Blocks

Normal force between blocks:

N₁ = weight of top block = 5kg

N₁ = 5 × 9.8 = 49 N

F₁ = μN₁ = 0.2 × 49 = 9.8 N

---

# Step 4: Friction Between Bottom Block and Ground

Normal force on 10 kg block includes BOTH masses:

N₂ = (5 + 10)kg

N₂ = 15 × 9.8 = 147 N

F₂ = μN₂ = 0.2 × 147 = 29.4 N

---

# Step 5: Total Friction Force

F_total = F₁ + F₂

F_total = 9.8 + 29.4 = 39.2 N

---

# Step 6: Apply Newton’s Second Law

F_net = ma

Net force:

F_net = Applied force − friction

F_net = 45 − 39.2 = 5.8 N

---

# Step 7: Solve for Acceleration

a = F_net / m

a = 5.8 / 10

a = 0.58 m/s²

---

# Final Answer

a ≈ 0.58 m/s²

---

# Key Insights

* Two friction forces act on the 10 kg block
* The top block increases the normal force → increases friction
* Always include ALL contact surfaces

---
