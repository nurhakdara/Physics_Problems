# Exercise 5: Inelastic Collision

 ## Problem

A 70 kg runner moving at 3 m/s jumps onto a 140 kg stationary cart.

Find:

1. Final speed of the runner + cart
2. Is kinetic energy conserved?

---

# Step 1: Identify the Type of Collision

This is a **perfectly inelastic collision**:

* The runner **sticks to the cart**
* They move together after collision

---

# Step 2: Use Momentum Conservation

Momentum is always conserved in collisions:

m₁v₁ + m₂v₂ = (m₁ + m₂)v_f

---

## Variables

* m₁ = 70 kg (runner)
* v₁ = 3 m/s (runner speed)
* m₂ = 140 kg (cart)
* v₂ = 0 m/s (cart at rest)
* v_f = final velocity

---

# Step 3: Substitute Values

(70 × 3) + (140 × 0) = (70 + 140)v_f

210 = 210 v_f

---

# Step 4: Solve

v_f = 210 / 210

v_f = 1 m/s

---

# Final Answer (Speed)

v = 1 m/s

---

# Step 5: Check Kinetic Energy

## Initial Kinetic Energy

KE_initial = (1/2)m₁v₁²

KE_initial = (1/2)(70)(3²)

KE_initial = 35 × 9 = 315 J

---

## Final Kinetic Energy

KE_final = (1/2)(m₁ + m₂)v_f²

KE_final = (1/2)(210)(1²)

KE_final = 105 J

---

# Step 6: Compare Energies

Initial KE = 315 J
Final KE = 105 J

Energy lost = 210 J

---

# Final Answer (Energy)

Kinetic energy is NOT conserved

---

# Explanation

In an inelastic collision:

* Momentum is conserved
* Kinetic energy is NOT conserved

The lost energy is transformed into:

* Heat
* Sound
* Deformation

---

If objects **stick together**:
→ Always use momentum
→ Expect energy loss
