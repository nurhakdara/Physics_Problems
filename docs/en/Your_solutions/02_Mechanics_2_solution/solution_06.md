# Exercise 6: Energy Dissipation

## Problem

A tennis ball is dropped from a height of 2.0 m.
After each bounce, it loses 30% of its mechanical energy.

Find:
→ Height after the **second bounce**

---

# Step 1: Understand the Physics

* Mechanical energy is proportional to height:

E = mgh

Since **m** and **g** are constant:

E depends h

---

# Step 2: Energy Loss per Bounce

* The ball loses **30% energy**
* So it keeps **70% of its energy**

Remaining energy factor:

0.7

---

# Step 3: First Bounce Height

Initial height:

h₀ = 2.0 m

After first bounce:

h₁ = 0.7 × h₀

h₁ = 0.7 × 2.0

h₁ = 1.4 m

---

# Step 4: Second Bounce Height

Apply the same factor again:

h₂ = 0.7 × h₁

h₂ = 0.7 × 1.4

h₂ = 0.98 m

---

# Final Answer

Height after second bounce:

h ≈ 0.98 m



h₂ = 2.0 × (0.7)² = 0.98 m

---

# Important Concept

Energy loss affects height because:

E = mgh

So:

* Less energy → lower height
* Same percentage loss → exponential decrease
