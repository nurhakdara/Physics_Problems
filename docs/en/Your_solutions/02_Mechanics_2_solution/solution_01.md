Exercise 1: 
A simple pendulum has a period of 4 seconds on Earth. What would its period be on the Moon, where the gravitational acceleration is about 1/6th of Earth's?

What is the required length of a simple pendulum to have a period of exactly 1 second on Earth?



Formula:
T = 2π √(L / g)

Period increases if length increases
Period increases if gravity decreases

Where:
T = period (s)
L = length (m)
g = gravity (m/s²)


Part 1: Period on the Moon


So:

T_moon / T_earth = √(g_earth / g_moon)

Given:

T_earth = 4 s
g_moon = g_earth / 6

We can say:

T_moon = 4 × √6 ≈ 9.8 s

Final Answer:
T ≈ 9.8 s



Part 2: Length for T = 1 s

Rearrange formula:
L = (g T²) / (4π²)

So:
L = (9.8 × 1²) / (4π²)

L ≈ 0.25 m

Final Answer:
L ≈ 0.25 m
