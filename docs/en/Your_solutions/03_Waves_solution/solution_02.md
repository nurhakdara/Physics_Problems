 String Harmonics
   A guitar string is 64 cm long and has a fundamental frequency (one antinode) of 330 Hz.
   What is the speed of the wave on this string?

# Exercise: String Harmonics

## Given

Length of string:
L = 64 cm = 0.64 m

Fundamental frequency:
f₁ = 330 Hz

---

# Step 1: Key Concept (Fundamental Mode)

For a string fixed at both ends:

L = λ / 2

So:

λ = 2L

---

# Step 2: Find Wavelength

λ = 2 × 0.64

λ = 1.28 m

---

# Step 3: Use Wave Equation

v = fλ

Where:

* v = wave speed (m/s)
* f = frequency (Hz)
* λ = wavelength (m)

---

# Step 4: Calculate Speed

v = 330 × 1.28

v ≈ 422.4 m/s

---

# Final Answer

v ≈ 422 m/s

---

# Key Insight

* Fundamental mode → only **one antinode**
* String length = half a wavelength
* That’s why λ = 2L

---

# Quick Formula (Fundamental)

v = 2Lf
