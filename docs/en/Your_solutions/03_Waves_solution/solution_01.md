Wave Properties A sound wave in air has a frequency of 440 Hz.
If the speed of sound in air is 343 m/s, what is its wavelength? 
What is its wavelength in water, where the speed of sound is 1482 m/s?

# Exercise: Wave Properties

## Given

Frequency:
f = 440 Hz

Speed of sound in air:
v_air = 343 m/s

Speed of sound in water:
v_water = 1482 m/s

---

# Step 1: Wave Formula

v = fλ

Rearrange to find wavelength:

λ = v / f

Where:

* v = wave speed (m/s)
* f = frequency (Hz)
* λ = wavelength (m)

---

# Step 2: Wavelength in Air

λ_air = v_air / f

λ_air = 343 / 440

λ_air ≈ 0.78 m

---

# Step 3: Wavelength in Water

λ_water = v_water / f

λ_water = 1482 / 440

λ_water ≈ 3.37 m

---

# Final Answers

Wavelength in air:
λ ≈ 0.78 m

Wavelength in water:
λ ≈ 3.37 m

---

# Key Insight

* Frequency stays the **same** in different media
* Speed changes → wavelength changes

Faster medium → longer wavelength
